Beginners Routine

Sets & Reps

Start off by doing 10 to 15 reps of each exercise. Aim for 1 to 2 sets to start. As you build strength,
you can add extra sets, and also increase the weight.

  • What is a rep? A repetition (rep) is one complete exercise movement.
  • What is a set? A set is a certain number of reps. For instance, 10 to 15 reps make up 1 set.

1. Dumbbell single-arm rows

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 5-7 mins

2. Dumbbell shoulder press

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 7-10 mins

3. Dumbbell chest press

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 7-10 mins

4. Bicep curls

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 7-10 mins

5. Triceps extensions

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 7-10 mins

6. Lunge

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 7-10 mins

7. Squats

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 7-10 mins

8. Calf raises

Sets: 20 x 2
Time: 7-10 mins

Intermediate Routine

Complete 3–4 sets here, with a minimum of 10 reps and a maximum of 20.

1. Barbell back squat

Sets: 20 x 3
Time: 8-9 mins

2. Barbell deadlift

Sets: 20 x 3
Time: 8-9 mins

3. Bent-over row

Sets: 20 x 3
Time: 8-9 mins

4. Floor chest fly

Sets: 20 x 3
Time: 8-9 mins

Advance Routine

When you can solidly complete the beginner and intermediate routines, give the advanced routine a go. Here, you’ll challenge more of your balance, stability, and strength.
Aim for 3–4 sets of 8–15 reps, upping the weight accordingly.

1. Renegade row

Sets: 20 x 4
Time: 10 mins

2. Single-leg deadlift

Sets: 20 x 4
Time: 10 mins

3. Bulgarian split squat

Sets: 20 x 4
Time: 10 mins

4. Bench press

Sets: 20 x 4
Time: 10 mins
